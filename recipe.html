Nigerian Jollof Rice

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a beloved West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, and a blend of spices. It is famous for its rich taste, vibrant orange-red color, and smoky flavor that makes it a favorite at weddings, parties, and family gatherings.

Recipe Information

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 45 minutes

Servings: 6 people

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 5 medium tomatoes (blended)
  • 2 red bell peppers (blended)
  • 1 Scotch bonnet pepper (blended, optional for spice)
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1 large onion (half chopped, half blended)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable or sunflower oil
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 2 seasoning cubes (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups chicken or beef stock
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Optional: sliced onions and tomatoes for garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water until the water runs clear, then set aside.
  2. Blend tomatoes, bell peppers, Scotch bonnet, and half the onions until smooth.
  3. Heat oil in a large pot. Sauté chopped onions for 2 minutes, then stir in the tomato paste and fry for another 2 minutes.
  4. Pour in the blended tomato mixture, add curry, thyme, seasoning cubes, and bay leaf. Cook on medium heat until the sauce thickens and oil floats to the top.
  5. Add chicken or beef stock, stir well, and bring to a boil.
  6. Pour in the rice, mix well, reduce heat to low, cover tightly, and cook for 20–25 minutes.
  7. Check occasionally and stir from the bottom to prevent burning. Add a little water or stock if needed.
  8. Once rice is cooked and has a slightly smoky aroma, turn off heat. Remove bay leaf.
  9. Fluff the rice with a fork, garnish with sliced onions and tomatoes, and serve hot.

Tips & Notes

Tip: For that authentic “party Jollof” flavor, let the rice steam on low heat for the last 10 minutes without opening the pot too often.
